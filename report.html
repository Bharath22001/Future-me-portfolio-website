Introduction

The site depicts a version of myself 10 years in the future showcasing the journey I have taken over the years. It is a version of myself that has successfully honed my craft as a computer science major by pursuing further studies and eventually achieving my goal of being a Quantum Engineer at IBM. The site not only attempts to convey my story to potential employers, but to my present self as well. Therefore, this site not only acts as a self introduction to potential employers but also acts as a sneak peak for me to peer into the future.

Taking this vision into account, I have decided to curate a storytelling experience that mimics the idea of my future-self sending me text messages, pictures and videos of my achievements. This can be seen in the ‘About Me’ page where as the user scrolls through the messages on the smartphone, new messages will appear from my future-self about my career highlights throughout the years. Additionally, ‘Projects’ page will have smartphones playing media of the projects I have developed and ‘Contact Me’ page will have text bubbles floating around containing excerpts from reviews of me sent by colleagues over the years.

The structure of my website consists of 5 pages, following the flat structure appraoch where in my case all pages are directly accessible from the home page. The first page is the “Home” page which is responsible for capturing the visitor’s attention and giving a short summary of myself. The second page is the “About Me” page which contains a timeline with information of my journey over the next 10 years. The third page is the “Experience” page that showcases the proficiency percentages of the various programming languages that I am experienced with. Additionally, it also contains a section that lists the frameworks at my disposal with each framework tied to a popup that details what the framework is used for and my proficiency with it. The fourth page is the “Projects” page which showcases the various projects that I have done. The last page is the “Contact Me” page with the top half showing the various reviews left by others about me while also allowing visitors to upload their own reviews of me. The bottom half allows the users to contact me through either inputting their own email and details into the input box or through my social media.

Accessibility

The first way I have made my website accessible is through a clear and consistent navigation system. All of my website pages have the exact same navigation menu with clearly separated links to the various pages. Additionally, the navigation menu intelligently removes webpage links based on the page the viewer is currently on. This helps to reduce clutter in the navigation menu. Having a consistent and clear navigation menu will be incredibly useful for users with cognitive or learning disabilities by making it easier for them to find and access the content they need.

The second accessibility feature I have implemented is the usage of high contrast colours across all web pages which uses colours that have a sufficient difference in brightness and saturation between the background and foreground elements, such as text and images. This is helpful to those with visual impairments such as colour blindness, low vision and vision sensitivity by reducing discomfort and strain trying to differentiate elements. An example of this implementation can be found in the “Home” page where the overlay text that transitions from “Hello,” to “I’m Bharath” changes text colour from white to black in accordance to the changing background colour while scrolling.

The third accessibility feature is viewport size responsiveness. I have provided my website with the ability to restructure its size and element positionings automatically such that it can be viewed through any device regardless of size. This will be helpful to those with disabilities as it allows them to comfortably visit my webpage through their preferred device while still getting the full experience.


Usability

The first way that site usability was taken into account was through mobile responsiveness. As aforementioned, I have designed my navigation menu such that it is clutter-free with clearly separated links. Moreover, links are also hidden from the navigation menu based on the page the visitor is currently on. Usability was also taken into account by allowing the navigation menu to restructure into a hamburger navigation menu when it is detected that the visitor is viewing the page from small display sizes. This makes it such that on mobile devices, the navigation menu is hidden behind a hamburger button. This ensures that the navigation menu is unobstructive and consumes little real estate on the already small mobile viewport sizes.

The second usability feature is the use of prominent Calls-to-Action (CTAs) which directs users to take certain necessary steps. The CTAs found throughout my web pages are positioned clearly with the use of eye-catching colours or notifications. An instance of this can be seen in the “Contact Me” page where if the user fails to correctly fill any of the input boxes within the contact me section, there will be a notification prompting the user of their error.

The third usability is mobile friendliness and responsiveness. My website has the ability to automatically adapt itself to varying viewport sizes. Depending on the size, my website will also restructure certain elements to provide a pleasant viewing experience to the user by eliminating improper positioning of any elements that extend past the viewport bounds. An instance of this would be in my “Experience” page where the framework icons will normally be positioned in a single row on desktop viewport sizes. However on mobile devices, the framework icons will restructure into a 2 by 2 grid to ensure that all elements are appropriately positioned on the screen.

This can be seen from the “About Me” page where my timeline was not merely just presented fully at once, instead the visitor has to scroll to reveal more. Evaluation II

I believe that my site could benefit from incorporating animation javascript libraries such as "GSAP". The home page’s parallax scrolling is an example of this as, even though my current parallax scrolling effect is present and functions as envisioned, I believe that I could utilise animation libraries to instil additional interactivity and seamlessness. Animation libraries would also be greatly helpful in providing animation effects to other texts and images present in my website. Thus, greatly improving the story telling aspect of my website by bringing focus to the aspects of the webpage that needs attention when needed, improving the flow to the story being told.

I also believe that my website would stand to benefit from additional accessibility and usability features. My current website offers great assistance to those with visual impairments by intentionally using high contrast colours, legible typography, etc. This aspect of my work could be further improved by incorporating a text-to-speech reader as my website lacks this feature that is essential to certain individuals. Hence, next time, I would be more intentional with which aspects of the website that I place my focus on as I had spent too much time improving the animation and aesthetics of my webpage instead of catering key accessibility features to those in need of them. Usability could also be improved by offering dark mode support to users viewing my webpage from dim-lit rooms as this would curate a more pleasant browsing experience by eliminating eye strain.
